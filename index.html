No-Reference Rendered Video Quality Assessment:
Dataset and Metrics

(Temporary Homepage for Review)

Abstract

Quality assessment of rendered videos is crucial for benchmarking graphics algorithms and optimizing rendering settings in academia and industry. In scenarios where test videos cannot be perfectly aligned with references or where references are unavailable, the importance of no-reference video quality assessment (NR-VQA) methods becomes undeniably evident. However, existing NR-VQA datasets and algorithms primarily focus on camera-captured videos; their direct application to rendered videos can lead to biased predictions since rendered videos are more vulnerable to temporal artifacts. To address this, we collect a large rendering-oriented video dataset with subjective quality annotations. This dataset covers a broad range of 3D scenes and rendering settings, with annotations performed across various displays to closely reflect real-world application scenarios. Building on this, we introduce a novel NR-VQA metric that evaluates rendered video quality by analyzing image quality and temporal stability. We validate our metric against existing NR-VQA metrics across multiple datasets, with results demonstrating its superior performance on rendered videos and first-rate results on existing camera-captured videos. Finally, we demonstrate the practical utility of our metric in two real-world tasks in benchmarking supersampling methods and evaluating frame generation strategies.
